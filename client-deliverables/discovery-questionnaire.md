# Client Discovery Questionnaire

Use this in the first client meeting to understand the problem before proposing any solution.

---

## Stage 1 — Context
*Understand who they are and what they do*

- Tell me about your business and what you do day to day
- Who are the key people involved in this process?
- How long have you been doing it this way?
- How many people are affected by this process?

---

## Stage 2 — Current State
*Map exactly how things work today*

- Walk me through exactly what happens from start to finish
- What tools or systems do you use today?
- How many people are involved and what does each person do?
- How long does this process take end to end?
- Where do things most often go wrong?

---

## Stage 3 — Pain Points
*Identify what's broken or frustrating*

- What frustrates you most about the current process?
- What do you spend the most time on that you wish you didn't?
- What keeps you up at night about this?
- If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?

---

## Stage 4 — Impact
*Quantify the cost of the problem*

- What does it cost you when this goes wrong?
- How much time per week does your team spend on this?
- What opportunities are you missing because of this?
- If you could put a dollar amount on this problem, what would it be?
- Have you lost customers or revenue because of this?

---

## Stage 5 — Vision
*Understand what success looks like*

- If this was working perfectly, what would that look like?
- What would your team be able to do with the time saved?
- What does success look like 6 months after we fix this?
- Are there any constraints I should know about — budget, timeline, technology?
- Who needs to approve this project?

---

## Discovery Rules

1. **One question at a time** — never ask two questions at once
2. **Go deep before going wide** — exhaust one topic before moving to the next
3. **Listen more than you talk** — your job is to understand, not to sell
4. **Take notes** — document everything the client says
5. **Never propose a solution during discovery** — that comes after analysis

---

## Red Flags To Listen For

- "We've always done it this way"
- "It's complicated to explain"
- "Different people do it differently"
- "We have a spreadsheet for that"
- "We lose data sometimes"
- "It takes forever"
- "Only one person knows how to do this"

These are all signs of significant automation opportunity!
